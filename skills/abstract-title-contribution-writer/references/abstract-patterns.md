# Abstract Patterns

Use these as move sequences. Adjust length to venue limits.

## Method Paper

Moves:

1. Problem or capability need.
2. Specific limitation of existing methods.
3. Proposed method and core mechanism.
4. Evaluation setting.
5. Main result evidence.
6. Implication or contribution.

Risk:

- Do not present benchmark wins without explaining the method's mechanism or scope.

## Benchmark or Dataset Paper

Moves:

1. Evaluation gap.
2. Why existing resources are insufficient.
3. New benchmark, dataset, or protocol.
4. Resource design and scale.
5. Findings revealed by evaluation.
6. Release or community value.

Risk:

- Dataset statistics alone are not enough; include what the resource lets the community learn.

## Empirical Study

Moves:

1. Common practice, assumption, or unresolved question.
2. Evidence gap in prior work.
3. Controlled study design.
4. Main findings.
5. Practical implication.
6. Scope.

Risk:

- Avoid causal or universal language if the study is observational or narrowly scoped.

## Analysis or Diagnostic Paper

Moves:

1. Phenomenon.
2. Why current explanations are inadequate.
3. Diagnostic framework.
4. Key analysis result.
5. Consequence for models, evaluation, or practice.

Risk:

- Calibrate "explains" versus "is consistent with" based on evidence.

## Systems or Tooling Paper

Moves:

1. Practical bottleneck.
2. Requirement at scale.
3. System/tool design.
4. Measured speed, scale, cost, reliability, or usability.
5. Artifact or deployment implication.

Risk:

- Quantify only verified numbers and state the measured environment when relevant.

## Theory Paper

Moves:

1. Theoretical question or limitation of existing analysis.
2. Assumptions or setting.
3. Main theorem, bound, or characterization.
4. Consequence for algorithms, evaluation, or interpretation.

Risk:

- Do not imply empirical performance unless the paper contains experiments.

## Application Paper

Moves:

1. Domain problem.
2. Why generic methods or existing domain methods fall short.
3. Proposed adaptation or system.
4. Domain-relevant evaluation.
5. Impact within the domain.

Risk:

- Match the abstract's value proposition to domain evidence, not only generic ML metrics.

## Negative Result or Limitation Paper

Moves:

1. Widely held assumption or common practice.
2. Why it matters.
3. Test or analysis.
4. Negative or limiting result.
5. Constructive recommendation.

Risk:

- Keep the tone evidence-driven, not dismissive.
